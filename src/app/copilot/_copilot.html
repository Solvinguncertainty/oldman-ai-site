The Problem

Does your company use Microsoft Copilot?

Most large enterprises are now restricted to Microsoft Copilot for AI work. It’s the only platform their security teams have approved for sensitive data.

But Copilot out of the box is a generic chatbot — it doesn’t know your industry, your reporting standards, or how a defensible document should be structured. The result: staff still spend three days writing the reports Copilot could draft in twenty minutes. The platform is in place. The expertise to use it isn’t.

The Core Idea

The prompt is the asset.
The LLM is the engine.

A great prompt isn’t a request — it’s an encoded expert. When the structure, the constraints, the tone, the source-grounding rules, and the domain knowledge of a senior practitioner are compressed into a single reusable prompt, the model produces work that holds up to legal, regulatory, and audit scrutiny. The expertise lives in the prompt. The model just runs it. That’s why the same Copilot license your IT team already approved can produce dramatically different output in trained hands.

Three-day reports. Twenty-minute reviews.

What We Do

Three offerings. One discipline.

01

Custom Prompt Frameworks

We design constrained, repeatable prompts for the specific report types your team produces — the ones that take days, the ones that go to the regulator, the ones that have to follow a defensible structure. Your staff use them inside your own Copilot tenant. Nothing leaves your environment.

02

On-Site & Remote Training

Half-day and full-day sessions teaching your people how to write prompts that produce defensible work product. Not generic AI workshops — applied training built around your actual report formats, using your actual document templates.

Workflow Advisory
